# DineInGo Customer Research Guide
## Pre-Early Access Validation Strategy

---

## Purpose

Before implementing the enhancement roadmap, we need to validate assumptions with real customers. This guide outlines what to ask, how to ask it, and how to interpret responses to ensure DineInGo solves real problems that people will pay for.

---

## Research Objectives

1. **Validate Pain Points:** Confirm the problems we think exist actually matter to customers
2. **Test Willingness to Pay:** Understand which features justify deposits, fees, or subscriptions
3. **Prioritize Features:** Let customers tell us what to build first
4. **Identify Deal-Breakers:** Find friction points that would prevent adoption
5. **Gather Pricing Insights:** Determine acceptable price points for premium features

---

## Target Audience Segments

### Segment 1: Frequent Diners (Primary Users)
- **Profile:** Dine out 2-4+ times per month
- **Age:** 22-45 years old
- **Income:** Middle to upper-middle class
- **Tech-savvy:** Comfortable with apps and online payments
- **Sample Size:** 50-100 respondents

### Segment 2: Occasional Diners (Secondary Users)
- **Profile:** Dine out 1-2 times per month
- **Age:** 25-55 years old
- **Income:** Middle class
- **Tech adoption:** Moderate
- **Sample Size:** 30-50 respondents

### Segment 3: Restaurant Owners/Managers (B2B)
- **Profile:** Own or manage restaurants
- **Restaurant type:** Casual dining to fine dining
- **Size:** 20-200 seats
- **Current pain points:** No-shows, slow periods, operational inefficiency
- **Sample Size:** 20-30 respondents

---

## Research Methods

### Method 1: Online Survey (Quantitative)
- **Tool:** Google Forms, Typeform, or SurveyMonkey
- **Distribution:** Social media, email, WhatsApp groups
- **Duration:** 5-7 minutes to complete
- **Incentive:** Entry to win ₹500 dining voucher (10 winners)
- **Target:** 100+ responses

### Method 2: In-Depth Interviews (Qualitative)
- **Format:** 1-on-1 video or phone calls
- **Duration:** 20-30 minutes
- **Participants:** 15-20 people (mix of diners and restaurant owners)
- **Incentive:** ₹500 Amazon voucher per participant
- **Focus:** Deep dive into behaviors, frustrations, and desires

### Method 3: Focus Groups (Qualitative)
- **Format:** Group discussion (5-8 people)
- **Duration:** 60-90 minutes
- **Sessions:** 3-4 groups
- **Incentive:** ₹750 per participant + free dinner
- **Focus:** Feature reactions, pricing discussions, competitive comparison

### Method 4: Prototype Testing (Usability)
- **Format:** Interactive prototype walkthrough
- **Duration:** 30-45 minutes
- **Participants:** 10-15 people
- **Incentive:** ₹1000 per participant
- **Focus:** UI/UX, booking flow, pain points in current design

## Interview Discussion Guide

### For Diners (20-30 minutes)

**Introduction (2 min)**
- Thank you for your time
- We're building a new restaurant booking platform
- No right or wrong answers, just honest feedback
- Recording for notes only (if permission granted)

**Warm-up (3 min)**
- Tell me about the last time you dined out
- How did you choose the restaurant?
- How did you make the reservation?

**Pain Points Deep Dive (8 min)**
- Walk me through your typical booking process
- What frustrates you most about it?
- Have you ever had a bad experience with reservations?
- Tell me about a time you didn't show up for a reservation
- How do you feel about restaurants that take deposits?

**Feature Reactions (10 min)**
- Show prototype/mockups of key features
- What do you think of seeing the floor plan before booking?
- How would you feel about pre-ordering food?
- What about dynamic pricing (cheaper off-peak, more expensive peak)?
- Would you pay for priority features? Why or why not?

**Competitive Comparison (5 min)**
- What apps do you use now?
- What do they do well?
- What do they do poorly?
- What would make you switch?

**Wrap-up (2 min)**
- If you could wave a magic wand, what would the perfect booking experience look like?
- Anything else you'd like to share?

### For Restaurant Owners (20-30 minutes)

**Introduction (2 min)**
- Same as above

**Warm-up (3 min)**
- Tell me about your restaurant

**No-Show Problem (8 min)**
- How big of a problem are no-shows for you?
- What have you tried to reduce them?

## Focus Group Discussion Guide

### Session Structure (90 minutes)

**Introduction (10 min)**
- Welcome and introductions
- Ground rules (everyone speaks, no wrong answers)
- Overview of DineInGo concept

**Current Experience Sharing (20 min)**
- Go around the room: share your last dining/booking experience
- What worked well?
- What was frustrating?

**Feature Prioritization Exercise (25 min)**
- Present 10 key features on cards
- Each person ranks top 5 most valuable
- Group discussion on rankings
- Identify must-haves vs nice-to-haves

**Pricing Discussion (20 min)**
- Present different pricing models
- Discuss willingness to pay
- Identify acceptable price points
- Discuss deal-breakers

**Competitive Comparison (10 min)**
- What do competitors do well?
- Where are the gaps?
- What would make DineInGo better?

**Wrap-up & Final Thoughts (5 min)**
- Final questions
- Thank participants

## Prototype Testing Protocol

### Testing Flow (30-45 minutes)

**Setup (5 min)**
- Explain think-aloud protocol
- No help unless stuck
- Observe and take notes

**Task 1: Browse & Discover (5 min)**
- Find a restaurant for dinner tonight
- What do you notice?
- What's confusing?

**Task 2: Make a Booking (10 min)**
- Book a table for 4 people

- Complete the booking
- Observe friction points

**Task 3: Pre-Order Food (8 min)**
- Add items to your order

- What's unclear?

**Task 4: Manage Booking (5 min)**
- Find your booking
- Try to modify it
- Try to cancel it

**Debrief (7 min)**
- What did you like?
- What was confusing?
- What would you change?
- Would you use this app?

## Data Analysis Framework

### Quantitative Analysis (Survey Data)

**Key Metrics to Calculate:**
1. **Feature Demand Score:** Average rating × % who rated 4-5
2. **Willingness to Pay:** % who selected "Yes" or "Maybe"
3. **Price Sensitivity:** Distribution of acceptable price points
4. **Pain Point Severity:** Frequency of mentions × impact rating
5. **Competitive Gaps:** Features competitors lack that customers want

**Statistical Significance:**
- Need minimum 100 responses for reliable data
- Look for patterns with >60% agreement
- Segment analysis by demographics

### Qualitative Analysis (Interviews & Focus Groups)

**Coding Framework:**
1. **Pain Points:** Categorize by severity and frequency
2. **Feature Reactions:** Positive, neutral, negative
3. **Behavioral Patterns:** How people actually book vs how they say they book
4. **Emotional Triggers:** What causes frustration, delight, anxiety
5. **Objections:** Why people wouldn't use certain features

**Key Questions to Answer:**
- What problems are most painful?
- Which features generate excitement?
- What are the deal-breakers?
- What pricing is acceptable?
- What would drive adoption?

## Decision Matrix

### Feature Prioritization Formula

**Priority Score = (Customer Demand × Business Impact × Feasibility) / Development Effort**

Where:
- **Customer Demand:** 1-10 based on survey ratings
- **Business Impact:** 1-10 based on revenue/retention potential
- **Feasibility:** 1-10 based on technical complexity
- **Development Effort:** 1-10 (higher = more effort)

**Thresholds:**
- Score >7: Build immediately (Phase 1)
- Score 4-7: Build soon (Phase 2)
- Score <4: Defer or drop

### Go/No-Go Criteria for Early Access

**Launch if:**
- ✅ 70%+ of respondents rate core features 4-5/5
- ✅ 50%+ willing to pay deposits or fees
- ✅ 60%+ would switch from current solution
- ✅ 20+ restaurants commit to early access
- ✅ Clear differentiation from competitors identified

**Don't launch if:**
- ❌ <50% find core features valuable
- ❌ <30% willing to pay anything
- ❌ No clear competitive advantage
- ❌ <10 restaurants interested
- ❌ Major usability issues in prototype testing

## Important Reminders

1. **Ask Open-Ended Questions:** Don't lead respondents to answers you want
2. **Observe Behavior:** What people do matters more than what they say
3. **Validate Willingness to Pay:** Saying "yes" is different from actually paying
4. **Test with Real Money:** If possible, ask for small commitments (₹10 to join waitlist)
5. **Segment Your Audience:** Different users have different needs
6. **Don't Build Everything:** Focus on features that solve real, painful problems
7. **Iterate Quickly:** Research → Build → Test → Learn → Repeat
